Professional Biography

I’ve included a bit more information about who I am, what I’ve been doing recently, and my plans for the future below.

Diligent, reliable, organized

My ample work experience has allowed me to understand my strengths. I’m diligent, reliable, and organized. While working as a Project Manager in the Marketing Communications at ResMed, I was responsible for an average of 14 projects ranging from single page collateral revisions to multi-deliverable, multi-phased product launches. Six years after I left, I received a call from the principle of a marketing agency I had worked with while there. She offered me a Project Manager position with her company because she remembered me and the work that I did.

I earned the respect of the design teams and the Product Managers whom I worked with by gathering and prioritizing relevant information in a single source, defining reasonable schedules, monitoring progress, and communicating on a scheduled and ad hoc basis to alleviate any confusion about product specs or project timelines. After projects were completed, post mortem meetings were held to learn what worked and where there was room for improvement.

This Project Manager role also helped sharpen my ability to understand how to work with and learn from each member of a team to become a productive and cohesive whole.

Patience and grit

My most recent efforts have centered on nurturing twin daughters and preparing them for the world beyond the home. Raising the girls has been both supremely challenging and rewarding. It’s taken incredible effort and taught me a lot about patience and grit. During naptime and after bedtime, I started honing my technical skills to begin a career transition to web development.

Curious

My interest in coding was sparked when completing my Master’s degree in Library and Information Science. I focused on information organization and took a few intro web development classes and enjoyed the challenge of solving programming puzzles so much, I decided to make a transition to a web development career.

One of my recent projects is an asynchronous web app using the Fetch API and jQuery called Poems, computed. It queries a poetry API for poems by a particular poet, does a word frequency analysis on these poems, and translates the results into a Highcharts word chart.

Another recent app created using React and React Router necessitated querying the New York Times Best Sellers Lists API and the Open Library API to display the top children’s books and provide links to more information about each book. One of the challenges lay in getting this app working smoothly on my personal website. After researching the issue, and with a little help, I put together the right combination of a basename for my routes, the right code in an htaccess file, and the right specs in the package.json file to get this app working.

As a life-long learner, I’m always curious to learn more and different things. Being challenged keeps me engaged.
